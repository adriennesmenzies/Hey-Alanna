A note from the creator

I’ve been a caregiver to my partner through his cancer battle. I’ve watched my mother and my sister fight theirs. I have family members who have served this country, and during my time at Wounded Warrior Project, I saw firsthand how deeply a veteran’s family carries them.

The truth is, no one is asking about the caregivers. Whether you’re caring for a parent with dementia, a child with cerebral palsy, or a spouse fighting cancer — caregivers are some of the most overworked and underserved people alive. No one is checking in when the fear is loudest. No one is there when the guilt of needing a break hits, or when the grief starts, even if your person isn’t gone.

I built Alanna because I’ve seen it, lived it, and walked alongside it. Caregivers deserve affordable, constant support — available the moment they need it. Not a replacement for a therapist or even the people in their lives. Just someone to sit with them, judgment free, in the quiet moments when everyone else is asleep and they’re still holding it all.

— Adrienne Selena Menzies
